# N:1 HCF + Andi
*2026-04-22 | 25 min | [[Andi Schuster]], [[Priscila Wagner]], [[Linette Voller]], [[Tahlia]], [[Pat Santos]], [[Kunday]]*

## Summary
[[Andi]] pushed to remove or rebrand the "Help Center" label, arguing it signals an outdated 2020 mental model and undersells the AI-powered, integrated product the platform has become. Two actions were approved: an experiment removing the navigation label to measure user impact, and a 3-day naming sprint to explore alternatives. Pri flagged real navigability risks — 6–14% of users rely on the label to return home — and Linette raised lost functionality (print shortcuts, design school links) as a concern. Andi's position: the experiment cost is lower than the time spent debating it; run it and learn.

## Decisions
- **Experiment approved**: Remove "Help Center" navigation label, measure impact on user engagement, article findability, and navigation patterns — decision by [[Andi]]
- **3-day naming sprint approved**: [[Priscila Wagner]], [[Linette]], and [[Tahlia]] to explore naming options that reflect AI-powered, dynamic help experience — not a lengthy investigation, a focused sprint
- **Naming direction**: Consider consolidating "Help Center" and "Help Assistant" under a single term, possibly just "Help" — users don't distinguish between them in research

## Open Questions & Blockers
- **Experiment metrics not defined yet**: Need to lock success criteria before launching — what does "navigation impact" actually mean to measure?
- **Naming scope unclear**: Quick Claude-generated ideation from transcript, or full competitive analysis? Andi said quick; worth confirming.
- **SEO implications not assessed**: "Help Center" carries real search equity — any external rename needs an SEO brief before shipping.
- **Timeline**: Andi says not urgent but flags high opportunity cost of delay. Needs a concrete deadline to avoid drift.
- **Unified naming risk**: If HC and HA both become "Help", transfer flows break — users saying "go to Help" while already in Help creates circular UX.

## Context
- Usage data: 6–14% of users click the HC label to return to the homepage, typically after landing on articles from Google. They use it to reset searches or browse categories — not primary nav.
- Leadership mental model (2020): Help Center = static article library. Current reality: search, AI assistant, 1000+ articles, design school content, shortcuts, contact flows.
- Andi's framing: "We killed Help Center, here's Help Center 2.0" — using the rename as an internal signal of evolution, not just a UX change.
- [[Linette]] flagged: removing the label removes direct access to browse-only features (print shortcuts, design school tutorial links) not surfaced through search.
- Industry context: some competitors now separate "docs/knowledge base" from "contact support" rather than grouping under "help center" — worth checking in the sprint.
