## CC (Carbon Copy)
- **Status:** Active
- **Definition:** Carbon copy. Used to let a third party know about something. The term comes from the days before photocopiers and smartphones, where when people needed to make multiple copies of something, they would use sheets of carbon paper to transcribe their pen or typewriter key imprints to another sheet of paper underneath, thereby creating a carbon copy. In emails, ‘Ccʼ indicates forwarding a copy to others not directly addressed in the ‘Toʼ field. Also related is Bcc, which stands for blind carbon copy. This indicates forwarding a copy to others not directly addressed in the ‘Toʼ or ‘Ccʼ fields without the ‘Bccʼ recipients appearing in the received message header of the ‘Toʼ or ‘Ccʼ recipients.
- **Last updated:** 2026-03-23

## Fix Flow

- **Definition:** A structured, deterministic self-help path within Help Assistant that guides users through resolving a specific issue (e.g. missing designs, payment problems, print issues) without escalating to human support. - **Aliases:** FF
- **Notes:** Distinct from Genflows (which are LLM-generated/dynamic). Fix Flows are rule-based and scripted. Owned by the Contextual Self-Help team. Examples: Missing Designs FF, Payment Fix Flows, Print Fix Flows, Subscription Refund Fix Flow. CSAT is categorised under Self-Help CSAT (not Automation CSAT).
- **Last updated:** 2026-03-23

## Genflow
- **Status:** Active
- **Definition:** Short for Generative Flow. An LLM-powered, dynamic flow within Help Assistant that handles user issues generatively rather than via a fixed/scripted path. Can be agentic — performing actions on behalf of the user. Contrast with Fix Flows, which are deterministic.
- **Aliases:** Gen Flow, Generative Flow
- **Notes:** Owned by the Contextual Self-Help team. Examples: Refund genflow, Cancel Print genflow, Failed Payments genflow, Manage Print Order genflow. In analytics, identified by `flow_type = 'Generative Flow'` or `response_source = generative_flow`. In some flows, a Fix Flow Quick Action may be shown as part of a genflow — the distinction is not always clean.
- **Last updated:** 2026-03-23

## Next Gen Flow
- **Status:** Active
- **Definition:** An evolution of the Help Assistant flow architecture that combines existing Fix Flows and Genflows while incorporating reusable UI components. More interactive and capable than either standalone flow type.
- **Notes:** First launched as "Next Gen Manage Print Order Flow" (Jan 2026, experiment). Owned by the Contextual Self-Help team. See #uv-h225-next-gen-automations.
- **Last updated:** 2026-03-23

## Help Assistant
- **Status:** Active
- **Definition:** Canva's in-product AI-powered support tool that helps users self-resolve issues. Surfaces Fix Flows, Genflows, help articles, and Magic Answers. Escalates to human support when needed via the Contact Flow.
- **Renamed from:** Ask Canva
- **Do not call it:** Ask Canva (deprecated name)
- **Notes:** Owned by the Contextual Self-Help team. Related channels: #contextual-self-help, #uv-help-ai-platform.
